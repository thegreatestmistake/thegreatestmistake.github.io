Meet the Charities

Who they are

Cool Earth is a charity that works alongside indigenous villages to halt rainforest destruction. The Rally organisers have an existing relationship with the charity, and the first £500 we raise will go to Cool Earth. Cool Earth saves at-risk rainforest and shields millions of acres of neighbouring forest. They put local people back in control, giving them the resources they need to keep their forest intact.

Who they are

The Brain and Spine Foundation is the only UK wide charity providing information and support on the full range of neurological conditions. There are over 350 different conditions of the brain and spine and together they affect more than 1 in 6 people in the UK. These conditions affect people of all ages and in many cases have long term impacts without the prospect of a cure.

What they do

  • They never have and never will buy land. Instead they help the local communities take legal control of their forest.
  • By building better schools, better clinics and better incomes, they ensure rainforest protection goes hand in hand with better lives.
  • They focus our efforts on rainforest that is most at-risk and which would otherwise be destroyed within the next 18 months.
  • The protected forest forms a shield to make millions of acres of neighbouring forest inaccessible to loggers.
  • They promise that more than 90p in every £1 you donate will go straight to their projects.

What they do

  • Neurological conditions and associated treatments are often complex, raising many questions for the people affected and their families.
  • The Brain & Spine Foundation provides support and quality information to people affected by brain and spine conditions, in order to reduce anxiety and help inform their choices.
  • Their Helpline is a free service staffed by neuroscience nurses. The nurses can answer questions about all neurological conditions and can offer information and support on any medical or related social and emotional issues of concern.
  • The nurses can answer queries by telephone, letter or email as well as providing an opportunity for people to share their experiences and ask questions on the discussion forum.
